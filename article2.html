What I Learned This School Year

By Chloe Sullivan  |  Editor-in-Chief, The Torch
[ Ilustração colorida estilo arte estudantil ]

Every year I sit down to write this piece, and every year Smallville gives me more material than I could possibly fit into a single column. So here, in no particular order, is what twelve months of chasing stories, dodging Principal Kwan, and surviving junior year actually taught me.

Lesson 1: If it's weird, you must be in Smallville. I've stopped being surprised by anything anymore — strange lights in the cornfields, classmates with sudden "talents," livestock acting up during meteor showers. At this point, weird is just the local weather report.

Lesson 2: High school is a constant state of metamorphosis. Just be careful what you change into. People here don't just grow up — they transform, sometimes literally. I've watched friends become strangers and strangers become the closest thing I have to family. The trick is making sure whatever you become is still someone you recognize.

Lesson 3: The best stories are the ones nobody wants you to print. If there's one thing being on the Torch staff has taught me, it's that the truth is always worth chasing, even when half the town would rather you didn't.

Lesson 4: Friendship is the one constant. Whatever else changes in this town — and trust me, it changes a lot — the people who show up for you are the ones who matter.

Here's to another year of asking too many questions. Smallville, you never disappoint.

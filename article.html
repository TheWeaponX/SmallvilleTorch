Year in Review

By Jake Black  |  Smallville Torch Staff Writer
[ Foto: Jake Black ]

At the end of every year, newspapers print "year-in-review" articles reflecting on the top 10 news stories that affected their readers. As our school year draws to a close, the Smallville High Torch has conducted just such a survey, and below are listed what you, the SHS students, feel are the top 10 most important events of our school year.

1. The mysterious meteor debris found scattered across Mr. Kent's farm continues to be a topic of curiosity among students, with theories ranging from the scientific to the downright bizarre.

2. The football team's unexpected turnaround season, led by team captain Whitney Fordman, brought the Crows back into playoff contention for the first time in years.

3. The Torch itself underwent a major redesign this year, expanding its coverage beyond school sports to include investigative pieces on local happenings — much to the chagrin of certain school administrators.

4. Several students reported strange occurrences around town, fueling rumors that Smallville may be sitting on more than just farmland.

5. The Spring Formal saw record attendance, with the gym transformed into what many called "the best decorated dance Smallville has ever seen."

As we close the book on another eventful year at Smallville High, one thing remains certain: this town never runs out of stories worth telling. Here's to next year — may it be just as unpredictable.
